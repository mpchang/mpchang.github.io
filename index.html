Work Experience

Luminous Computing

05/2019 - 05/2023

I was employee #5, and led the photonics team at Luminous Computing. Our team delivered the first monolithically integrated 112 Gbps PAM4 transceiver chips, fabricated in the 300mm GlobalFoundries Fotonix platform. PhD

2011 - 2017

I received my PhD from the Lightwave Lab at Princeton University, with a research focus on building photonic integrated circuits for ultra wideband wireless signal processing. For my thesis, I designed the first integrated circuit that reduced wireless interference over 1000x over every LTE channel in existence at the time, using analog interference cancellation. The research attracted significant attention, and I co-founded a startup company called Rebeless to commercialize the technology. The company was not successful, and it shut down in 2017. At Princeton, I was a recipient of the National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate Fellowship and the Gordon Wu Fellowship.

Projects

Uncovering Missed Tackle Opportunities

Every year the NFL hosts a competition to develop new models and metrics to improve football analytics. Our team's submission contained three new metrics: tackle opportunity rate, missed opportunity rate, and tackle conversion rate. Compared to the traditional metric of total tackle count, tackle opportunity and tackle conversion rate allow us to shed insight on how defenders are actually earning their tackles. Meanwhile, the missed opportunity metric measures something that traditional metrics do not capture at all. To compute these metrics, we built a machine learning model that predicts, in real-time, the tackle probability of every defender on the field given player tracking data.
